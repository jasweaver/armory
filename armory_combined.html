Glossary & Reference
NFA Firearm Types
NFA — National Firearms Act (1934). Regulates the manufacture, transfer, and possession of certain firearms. Each NFA item requires a $200 tax stamp, ATF Form 4 (transfer) or Form 1 (make), and a background check. Wait times currently run 6–12+ months for Form 4.
SBR
Short-Barreled Rifle — a rifle with a barrel under 16" or overall length under 26". Requires NFA registration. A pistol-configuration AR with a brace is not automatically an SBR, but adding a stock converts it to one.e.g. 10.3" Daniel Defense MK18, 11.5" BCM upper on registered lower
SBS
Short-Barreled Shotgun — a shotgun with a barrel under 18" or overall length under 26". Same NFA process as SBR.e.g. Mossberg 590 cut to 14", Remington 870 TAC-14 configured as SBS
Suppressor
Also called a silencer. A device that reduces the report of a firearm. NFA-regulated regardless of the host firearm's classification. Form 1 (DIY) or Form 4 (commercial transfer).e.g. SilencerCo Scythe-Ti, OCL Hydrogen-S 6.5mm
AOW
Any Other Weapon — a catch-all NFA category for firearms that don't fit other definitions: pen guns, cane guns, pistols with a vertical foregrip, smooth-bore pistols, and certain concealable firearms. Transfer tax is only $5 (vs $200 for other NFA items).e.g. H&R Handy-Gun, smoothbore AR pistol with VFG
DD
Destructive Device — two categories: (1) explosive devices such as grenades, rockets, and mines; (2) large-bore firearms with a bore diameter exceeding 0.50 caliber not classified as sporting shotguns. $200 tax stamp.e.g. 37mm/40mm launcher (as DD), anti-tank rifles
MG
Machine Gun — any firearm that fires more than one round per trigger pull, or any part designed to convert a semi-auto to full-auto. Civilian transfer of post-May-1986 MGs is prohibited (Hughes Amendment). Pre-1986 registered MGs are transferable but extremely expensive.e.g. Pre-86 M16, registered Lightning Link, DIAS
Non-NFA Firearm Types
Pistol
A handgun designed to be fired with one hand. Barrel length is not regulated at the federal level for pistols. A firearm originally manufactured as a pistol can have a brace without NFA implications.e.g. Glock 17, 1911, AR-15 pistol configuration
Rifle
A long gun with a rifled barrel of 16" or more and overall length of 26" or more, designed to be fired from the shoulder. No NFA paperwork required as long as it stays in spec.e.g. AR-15 with 16" barrel, bolt-action precision rifle
Shotgun
A smoothbore long gun with a barrel of 18" or more and overall length of 26" or more, designed to be fired from the shoulder. Rifled-barrel shotguns firing slugs are also common.e.g. Mossberg 500, Remington 870, Benelli M4
NFA Process & Form Fields
Form 1
ATF form to make an NFA item yourself (e.g. converting a pistol lower to an SBR, or building a suppressor). Must be approved before manufacturing begins.Used for: SBR, SBS, Suppressor (DIY)
Form 4
ATF form to transfer an existing NFA item from a dealer or individual to you. The $200 tax stamp is paid at the time of submission. The item stays with the transferor until approved.Used for: all NFA transfers from FFL/SOT or individual
UPIN
Unique Personal Identification Number — issued by the FBI through the NICS Voluntary Appeal File (VAF) program. Allows you to include your UPIN on a Form 4473 to expedite NICS checks and reduce the chance of a delay or denial due to a false-positive match.Relevant for NFA transfers and FFL purchases
NFA Order #
The tracking or order number assigned by your dealer or trust for a pending NFA transfer. Not an ATF-assigned number — it's your FFL/SOT's internal reference used to track where your Form 4 is in the queue.Format varies by dealer
NFA Status
Three-state field tracking where an NFA item is in the approval process. Not Submitted means paperwork not yet filed. Submitted / Pending means the ATF Form 1 or Form 4 has been filed and you are waiting on approval. Approved means the tax stamp has been received and the item is legal to possess. The home dashboard "NFA Pending" count reflects all items not yet approved.Items approved drop off the NFA Pending list on the home screen
Gun Trust
A legal trust (NFA trust) used to hold NFA items. Allows named co-trustees to possess items without the primary owner present. Avoids the CLEO signature requirement for individual transfers in some jurisdictions.Trustees must be listed on Form 4 / Form 1
Photos, Documents & Storage
Photo 1 & 2
Up to two photographs per firearm. Accepts common image formats (JPG, PNG, HEIC, etc.). Thumbnails display in the attachment slot. Recommended uses: overall condition at time of purchase, serial number close-up, engraving documentation for NFA items.Click the photo pill in the table row to view; click the slot overlay to replace or remove
Gear Photo 1 & 2
Up to two photographs per gear item (including uppers, optics, and accessories). Useful for insurance claims, condition history, and serial/marking snapshots.Shown as a photo pill in the Gear table Attachments column
Attachments Column
Firearms and Gear tables include an Attachments column showing pills when files are present. Click a pill to open the viewer, then use Download for a local copy.If a row has no attachments, the column displays —
ATF Form
Attach the approved ATF Form 1 or Form 4 for NFA items. Accepts PDF (recommended) or image scan. Having the approved form digitally accessible is useful when transporting NFA items and for trust documentation purposes.Accepts: PDF, JPG, PNG — click ATF pill in table to view inline
IndexedDB
The storage engine used by this app. Unlike localStorage (5–10 MB limit), IndexedDB can store binary files like photos and PDFs with a limit tied to available disk space — typically gigabytes. All data stays on your device and is never transmitted anywhere.Data persists across browser sessions; clear browser site data to reset
Backup
Use Export CSV regularly to back up firearms, ammo, and gear records as text data. Note that CSV/PDF exports do not include binary attachments (photos, ATF forms, or document files) — those live in IndexedDB. To back up attachments, open each file in the viewer and use Download.Recommended: export CSV monthly and after major changes
Important: Clearing your browser's site data, cookies, or storage for this app's origin (e.g. localhost:8080) will permanently delete all records and attachments from IndexedDB. Always keep external backups via CSV export and downloaded attachment files.
Gear & Accessories
Magazine
Track caliber, capacity, manufacturer, model, quantity on hand, and which firearm(s) they're compatible with. Condition field flags mags that need replacement. Retire condition is highlighted in red.e.g. Magpul PMAG Gen3 30rd 5.56, Lancer AWM 20rd 6.5 CM
Barrel
Records caliber, length, twist rate, profile (government, pencil, heavy, bull), and threading. Twist Rate is tracked in its own field (not in Caliber / Spec).e.g. 18" 22 ARC 1:7.7, 10.3" 5.56 gov 1:7
Optic
Tracks magnification range, reticle type, mount type, and recorded zero distance. Compatible firearm field links the optic to the specific host it's mounted on.e.g. Vortex Razor HD Gen III 1-10x24 EBR-7C, Sig Romeo5
Trigger
Records pull weight, type (single-stage / two-stage), and compatible platform. Useful for tracking drop-in cassettes across multiple lowers.e.g. Geissele SSA-E 2-stage ~3.5lb, LaRue MBT-2S
Light / Laser
Tracks output (lumens or IR wavelength), battery type, mount/rail system, and pressure switch model. Battery field helps ensure you stock the right cells.e.g. Surefire M300V 500lm CR123, Mawl-C1+ IR 850nm
Stock / Brace
Use for buttstocks, braces, and related components. Track platform compatibility and fitment notes in Caliber / Spec and Notes fields.e.g. SBA3 brace, B5 SOPMOD stock
Upper
Use for AR-pattern uppers. Select Receiver Only when tracking a stripped/assembled receiver without barrel/BCG, or Complete Upper for full assemblies shipped as complete units. Upper Length (in) tracks assembly length separately from caliber/spec.e.g. Complete 11.5" 5.56 upper, receiver-only 6mm ARC build base
Length (in) Column
Gear table includes a shared Length column that auto-populates from Barrel Length for Barrel items and Upper Length for Upper items. Other gear types display — in this column.Use it to sort mixed barrel/upper inventories by length
Bipod / Tripod
Track support systems for precision shooting. Use Caliber / Spec for leg range, mount type, or head style.e.g. 6-9" swivel bipod, tripod with ARCA head
Case / Bag
Use for hard cases, soft cases, and range bags. Caliber / Spec can store dimensions or carry capacity.e.g. 36" hard case, 2-rifle drag bag
Cleaning
Use for rods, jags, solvents, brushes, and maintenance kits. Caliber / Spec can list supported calibers.e.g. 5.56 bore snake, universal cleaning kit
Other (Gear Type)
Catch-all type for accessories that do not fit the predefined categories. Use Notes for details and intended host firearm.e.g. torque wrench, armorer tool, bench block
Condition
Four states: New (unused), Good (serviceable), Worn (functional but degraded), and Retire (needs replacement). The stats bar tracks "Needs Replacement" count. Use Retire to flag magazines with cracked feed lips, springs, or worn followers.Retire count shown in stats bar with amber warning
Documents Vault
Government / ATF
UPIN letter from the FBI NICS VAF program, approved ATF Form 4 tax stamps, approved ATF Form 1s, and any other federal correspondence related to your firearms or NFA items.e.g. UPIN letter, approved Form 4 for suppressor
Permits & Licenses
Virginia Concealed Handgun Permit (CHP) and any other state-issued carry or ownership permits. Photograph or scan the card. The expiry date field will flag permits expiring within 90 days.e.g. Virginia CHP, out-of-state non-resident permits
Trust Documents
The NFA gun trust legal instrument, any trust amendments, and letters acknowledging trustee additions or removals. Keeping a digital copy ensures Michelle as successor trustee can access the document if needed.e.g. Trust instrument, amendment adding co-trustee
Purchase Records
Dealer invoices and sales receipts from FFL transfers. Note: the ATF Form 4473 stays with the FFL and is not a document you possess. However your dealer's own receipt or invoice is yours to keep.e.g. Dealer invoice, online retailer order confirmation
Insurance
Firearms insurance policy documents, appraisal certificates for high-value items, and scheduled item riders. The expiry date field is useful for tracking annual policy renewals.e.g. NRA Carry Guard policy, scheduled item appraisal
Other (Document Category)
Use when a file does not map cleanly to a built-in document category. Keep the title and notes specific so search and filtering remain useful.e.g. warranty letter, gunsmith service notes, serial verification sheet
Expiry Alerts
Documents with an expiry date set will be flagged in the table. ⚡ means expiring within 90 days; ⚠ means already expired. The stats bar shows counts for both. Use this for CHP renewals and insurance policy renewals.CHP: valid 5 years in Virginia; renew 90 days before expiry
Ammunition Bullet Types
FMJ
Full Metal Jacket — lead core enclosed in a harder metal (usually copper) jacket. Does not expand on impact. Standard range/training ammo. Generally cheaper per round.Best for: range practice, training, semi-auto reliability
JHP
Jacketed Hollow Point — a jacketed bullet with a cavity in the tip that causes it to expand on impact, increasing the wound channel and reducing over-penetration risk. Standard defensive ammo.Best for: self-defense, home defense, carry
HP
Hollow Point — same expansion concept as JHP but may lack a full jacket. Common in rimfire (.22 LR) and some pistol loads.Best for: small game, defensive rimfire loads
SP
Soft Point — a jacketed bullet with an exposed lead tip. Expands more than FMJ but less aggressively than a hollow point. Common in hunting rifle rounds.Best for: hunting, medium/large game
Match / OTM
Open-Tip Match — a precision bullet where the tip is open due to manufacturing (drawing from the base), not for expansion. Optimized for consistent ballistic performance and accuracy at range.Best for: precision rifle, long-range competition
Subsonic
Ammunition loaded to keep the projectile below the speed of sound (~1,125 fps). Eliminates the supersonic crack, making it significantly quieter when used with a suppressor.Best for: suppressed hosts, especially .300 BLK and 9mm
Tracer
Projectile includes a pyrotechnic compound that burns in flight, creating a visible trajectory trace. Useful for training and observation but restricted in some ranges/jurisdictions due to fire risk.Best for: trajectory observation, low-light drills (where legal and permitted)
Frangible
Bullets designed to disintegrate on impact with a hard surface. Reduces ricochet risk and lead contamination. Common for steel target shooting at close range and indoor ranges.Best for: close-range steel, indoor ranges
Slug
Single large shotgun projectile (one projectile per shell), used for longer shotgun distances and greater penetration than shot loads.Best for: shotgun hunting, defensive slug loads, extended-range shotgun use
Buckshot
Shotgun load containing multiple large pellets (e.g., 00 buck). Common defensive load with strong close-range stopping power.Best for: defensive shotgun use, close-range applications
Birdshot
Shotgun load containing many small pellets optimized for birds and small game. Lower penetration than buckshot/slugs.Best for: bird hunting, clay targets, small game
Other (Bullet Type)
Catch-all for specialty rounds not represented by preset options. Use Product Name / SKU and Notes to capture exact load details.Examples: wadcutter, bonded soft point variants, specialty defensive loads
CPR
Cost Per Round — calculated automatically. Enter Total Paid and Total Rounds Purchased for bulk buys (e.g. $500 for 1,000 rounds = $0.500/rd). No need to break it down by box. Rounds Per Box is optional and only used for the box count display and +/− button step size. For legacy records with Price Per Box entered, CPR falls back to Price ÷ Rds/Box.Adjust By field lets you set a custom +/− step — e.g. 50 rds per range session
Tab Field Values (Quick Reference)
Firearms: Type
Allowed values: Pistol, Rifle, Shotgun, SBR, SBS, Suppressor, Machine Gun, AOW, DD.NFA item logic applies automatically to NFA types and/or when NFA Order # is present
Firearms: NFA Form Status
Allowed values: Not Submitted, Submitted / Pending, Approved.Home "NFA Pending" and firearms stats include items not yet approved
Ammo: Bullet Type
Allowed values: FMJ, JHP, HP, SP, Match / OTM, Subsonic, Tracer, Frangible, Slug, Buckshot, Birdshot, Other.Use Other plus Notes for uncommon projectile types
Ammo: Stock Status
Derived values: In Stock, Low Stock, Out of Stock.Low/Out are based on Quantity on Hand and Low Stock Alert threshold
Documents: Category
Allowed values: Government / ATF, Permits & Licenses, Trust Documents, Purchase Records, Insurance, Other.Expiry alerts are independent of category and only require Expiry Date
Gear: Type & Condition
Type values: Magazine, Upper, Barrel, Optic, Trigger, Stock / Brace, Light / Laser, Bipod / Tripod, Case / Bag, Cleaning, Other. Condition values: New, Good, Worn, Retire.Retire items feed "Needs Replacement" count
Gear: Upper Type
Allowed values (when Type = Upper): Receiver Only, Complete Upper.Displayed in the dedicated Upper Type column and included in Gear CSV/PDF exports
Gear: Length Fields
Barrel items use Barrel Length (inches). Upper items use Upper Length (inches). Both flow into Gear table Length (in).This keeps caliber/spec and length separate for cleaner records
Firearm Measurements
Bbl (in)
Barrel Length in inches — measured from the bolt face (chamber) to the muzzle. Critical for NFA compliance: rifles must be 16"+ and shotguns 18"+ to stay non-NFA. Measured with the action closed.Legal minimums: Rifle 16", Shotgun 18"
OAL (in)
Overall Length in inches — total length of the firearm from end to end with the stock fully extended. Secondary NFA threshold: long guns under 26" OAL may be classified as SBR/SBS regardless of barrel length.Legal minimum for non-NFA long gun: 26"
